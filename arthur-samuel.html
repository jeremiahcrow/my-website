Early Life

Arthur Samuel was born in 1901 in Emporia, Kansas. He studied electrical engineering at MIT and later earned his master's at MIT as well.

His early interests in computing eventually led him to pioneering work in artificial intelligence at IBM.

Major Contributions

Samuel created a checkers-playing program in the 1950s that improved its performance through experience, a landmark in AI.

He coined the term "machine learning" to describe this process of programs improving with experience.
