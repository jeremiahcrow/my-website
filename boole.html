George Boole: Father of Boolean Logic

George Boole (1815–1864) was a mathematician and logician whose algebra laid the foundation for modern computing.

Early Life

Boole was born in Lincoln, England. Largely self-taught, he showed talent in mathematics and languages before becoming a professor at Queen’s College, Cork.

Major Contributions

His invention of Boolean algebra reduced logic to equations using values of 0 and 1, forming the basis for digital circuits.

Contributions to AI

Boolean logic underpins rule-based systems, search engines, and AI decision-making processes that rely on true/false operations.

Legacy

Every digital computer operates on Boolean principles. Without Boole, modern programming and AI systems would not exist.
