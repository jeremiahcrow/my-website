George Boole: The Father of Boolean Logic

George Boole (1815–1864) was a mathematician whose work laid the foundation for digital logic and modern computing.

Early Life

Born in Lincoln, England, Boole was largely self-taught. He excelled in mathematics and became a professor at Queen's College, Cork.

Major Contributions

Boole’s book *The Laws of Thought* introduced Boolean algebra, which uses binary true/false logic.

Impact on AI

Boolean logic is essential for digital circuits and AI reasoning systems. Without it, modern computing and machine learning would not exist.

Legacy

Every search engine query, computer program, and AI model still relies on Boolean logic at its core.
