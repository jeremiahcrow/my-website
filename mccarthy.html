Early Life & Education

Born in 1927, McCarthy studied mathematics at Caltech and Princeton. He became a professor at MIT and later at Stanford, where he founded the AI lab.

Major Contributions

McCarthy organized the 1956 Dartmouth Conference, marking the birth of AI as a field. He also invented LISP, the programming language of choice for AI research for decades.

AI Foundations

He introduced concepts such as time-sharing systems and formalized AI research, establishing many of the principles that guided future advancements.
