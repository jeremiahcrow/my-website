Early Life & Education

Born in 1901, Samuel studied electrical engineering at MIT. Later, he joined IBM where he began exploring computing and artificial intelligence.

His engineering background equipped him with the skills to develop innovative programs that could learn from experience.

Major Contributions

Samuel developed the first checkers-playing program that improved as it played more games, a groundbreaking achievement in adaptive algorithms.

He also coined the term machine learning in the 1950s, describing the process by which machines could improve performance without explicit programming.
