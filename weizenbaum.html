Joseph Weizenbaum: Creator of ELIZA

Weizenbaum is best known for creating ELIZA, one of the first natural language processing programs, simulating conversation like a psychotherapist.

Early Life & Education

Born in 1923 in Germany, Weizenbaum fled Nazi persecution and moved to the U.S. He studied mathematics and worked in early computing at Wayne State University and MIT.

Major Contributions

In 1966, Weizenbaum built ELIZA, which could mimic human conversation using pattern matching. The program shocked the world by showing how easily humans could bond with machines.

AI Foundations

ELIZA became one of the first chatbots, inspiring research into human-computer interaction and natural language processing.
